The Opportunity

The Linux Threat Analysis project was undertaken to address a shortage of study material for analyzing victimized Linux-based machines. Usually, Linux-based machines are used for security investigation anaylsis and Windows-based systems are the victim machines getting attacked. The project was to provide WelTec courses with educational material that could be used in assessing the forensics skills of cybersecurity students. Its product could be also available on Digital Corpora, a global respository of similar datasets.

Photography by freestocks • Development

The Process

The methodology for the project was Scrumban. This combined important features of two propular methodologies: Scrum and Kanban. Linux Threat Analysis implemented Scrumban which merged the structure and predicatability of Scrum with Kanban's flexibility and continuous workflow.

Photography by Kelly Sikkema• Scrumban

The Result

A realistically designed, Linux-based computer crime simulated in a virtual environment configured to represent enterprise-level network security for a digital forensics investigation that furthers cybersecurity skills, education, and research.

About The Project

Linux Threat Analysis a project undertaken by Team T.Y.P.O., a group of I.T. students looking to contribute up-to-date material for computer forensics education at WelTec. It was based entirely on Linux systems to address a shortage of similar datasets.
