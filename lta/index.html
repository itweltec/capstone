AN EXPANSIVE DATASET AT YOUR DISPOSAL

Questions

Simple and extended scenario backgrounds with focus question sets and broader challenges for assessment.

Evidence

Memory images, network traffic captures, disk images, and log files for students to dig through and examine.

Solutions

Worked answers and analyses for each focus question and challenge to help instructors when marking.

DESIGNED TO DEVELOP KEY SECURITY SKILLS

Evidence Handling

Learn how to efficiently discover telling artifacts left over in computer memory and network traffic despite heavy data saturation.

Forensic Reporting

Draw from a wide range of data sources to communicate your findings in a written report that answerings pre-prepared prompts.

Linux-based Analysis

Focus on understanding what common threats and exploits look like when exacted on UNIX-like servers, workstations, and network devices.

A REALISTIC ENVIRONMENT

Jump into a fully configured corporate network with a topology that mirrors enterprise, including firewalls, intrusion detection systems, DNS servers, an internal mail server, file server, logging and caching capabilities, and more.

A RELEVANT THREAT

Be a part of an elaborate storyline specifically designed to mimic attacks orchestrated by real people in real networks. See remnants of reverse shell exploitation, password cracking, DNS tunneling, and data exfiltration enacted by carefully manufactured personas.

About The Project

Linux Threat Analysis a project undertaken by Team T.Y.P.O., a group of I.T. students looking to contribute up-to-date material for computer forensics education at WelTec. It was based entirely on Linux systems to address a shortage of similar datasets.
