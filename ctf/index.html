The Problem & The Drive

The background and context which drove the team, and this project.

The field of CyberSecurity in today's technological and interconnected world, has seen a great spike in popularity, relevance and demand. Yet there are many who lack a true insight into what this field has to offer. They then hesitant to join in the study and practices of this field, due to the lack of accurate knowledge. Many still assume it all just boils down to "The Matrix" style of hacking.

The goal of this project was to create a comprehensive and informative Cybersecurity learning experience in the form of fun, exciting, and even challenging, Capture the flag (CTF) challenges. Capture the flag is an IT game that requires competitors to utilize different tools, and techniques to recover hidden “flags”, the players would then enter their found flags into the CTFd.io website (Or whichever CTF service is being used) to confirm that they have the correct flag and score points.

The desired outcome of this project is for participants to gain knowledge, potential understanding, and a clearer insight into what the vast CyberSecurity field has to offer, by performing and completing the tasks, and ultimately, cultivate an interest in the expanding field of CyberSecurity.

The Solution

Our proposed solution to the aforementioned problem

As stated above, the team would design and develop a series of CTF challenges that are educational, interactive, and fun. They will be hosted on the ctfd.io website, run for varying amounts of time, and are based on the core disciplines of cybersecurity. Which include digital forensics, web-based, cryptography, social engineering, network-based, and many more. The challenge types can range from things such as jeopardy style or attack and defense. The Client wishes to employ some of these challenges during WelTec open days, in an attempt to increase awareness, and giving a taster of what the cybersecurity field has to offer.

Aside from the varying topics being covered, these modules, and their respective questions, will have varying diffculties, so as to create a healthy mix of learning and challenge, and cater to varying degrees of knowledge. Documentation will also accompany the modules. They will range from the narratives, which provide a story context for each of the modules and the flags participants are hunting for, and the instructional documents which have been written to allow for anyone to easily follow along, or if teachers wish to use the modules strictly for learning purposes and less of a challenge. Hints are also provided if they are needed, but many come at the cost of points.

The Client



The Client and main contact point for any Client-based information for this project was Mr. Paul Bryant, a senior Information Technology lecturer at Wellington Institute of Technology (WelTec), who had requesting the product on behalf of WelTec’s School of IT. This is so the institute may use the product at their open days to encourage high-school level students (and anyone with no prior experience of the cybersecurity field) to join WelTec, particularly the cybersecurity field. As a senior lecturer Paul reports to both the Head of School and the Program Manager.
