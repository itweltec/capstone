The Scenario

"Cake It Forward 2020" - A story of confidential company data smuggled from the inside out.

The Company

Pineapple Information Technology Solutions (PITS) is a start up company that develops bespoke software for a variety of clients. The organisation generates most of its revenue through its projects. One such project, "XWare", is a highly confidential endeavour headed by a small team of employees.

The Incident

As part of ongoing operational security (OPSEC) measures at PITS, the security team monitors common data leakage indicators and depositories. Recently, OPSEC practices revealed that one such storage point, a pastebin website, was found to be publicly hosting XWare project data that could only be found on the XWare directory share. The only non-privileged user with the rights to modify that share is the XWare project manager, George Gatsby.

The Evidence

In response to resulting suspicions, the PITS security team set up a listening device to record packet captures of network activity over the next two days. They also captured the memory of the managerâ€™s machine on each of those days. Therefore, there are two packet captures of mirrored internal network traffic, and two memory images of the project manager's workstation available for examination. After the two capture days, several other data sources were investigated, resulting in disk images, log files, and other memory files being retrieved.

THE SUSPECTS

George

The XWare project manager and only non-privileged user with access to the compromised file shares

Alison

A software developer on the XWare team working under George's leadership.

Marcus

A junior I.T. administrator with greater privileges and powers in the network than your average user.
